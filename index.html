Course Handicap

Your Course Handicap is the number of strokes you receive on a specific course and tee box. It is different from your Handicap Index.

It is calculated as:
Index × (Slope ÷ 113) + (Rating − Par)

9-Hole Play
Enter your Course Handicap for 9 holes — not 18. Each nine at Nemadji has its own rating and slope, so your 9-hole course handicap will be different from your full 18-hole number.
Tee Box Dependent
Your Course Handicap changes depending on which tees you play. Always use the number from the tee box you are playing tonight — Black/White, White, etc.

Calculate yours using the USGA's official tool:
→ ghin.com — log in and your Course Handicap is calculated automatically for any course and tee box.

Handicap Index

Your Handicap Index is a portable number that represents your potential ability as a golfer. It's calculated by the USGA from your recent scores and is the same no matter where you play.

Think of it as your "travel-ready" handicap — you bring it with you to any course, and that course converts it into a Course Handicap based on its difficulty.

Index vs. Course Handicap
An Index of 12.4 might become a Course Handicap of 14 from the back tees, or 11 from the forwards. Same player, same Index — different strokes given.

Get your official Index through the USGA:
→ ghin.com — log in to see your current Handicap Index.

Playing Off

In match play, the lowest-handicap player plays off scratch — they get no strokes. Everyone else receives the difference between their Course Handicap and the lowest one.

Example
If course handicaps are 3, 7, 9, 12, the lowest is 3 — so they play off scratch. The others receive 4, 6, and 9 strokes respectively.

Strokes are allocated to the holes by stroke index — the hardest holes first.
